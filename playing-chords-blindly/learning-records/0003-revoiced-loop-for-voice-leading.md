# Re-voiced the four-chord loop to first inversions (classic voice leading)

Eduard flagged that the loop's **G→Am** change (V→vi) moved G·B·D → A·C·E as a
parallel block, making **parallel perfect fifths** between the outer voices —
discouraged in common-practice voice leading — and the leading tone (B) never
resolved up to C. He asked to fix the chord forms so the student builds the
recommended habit, **without** adding any voice-leading theory to the lesson text
(irrelevant to the lesson's goal).

## Decision
The four chords are an interlocked hand-sized box: G→Am can't be fixed in isolation
without breaking the C→G glued finger or forcing an octave jump. So the whole loop
was re-voiced to first/second inversions.

    OLD:  C = G C E · G = G B D · Am = A C E · F = A C F
    NEW:  C = C E G · G = B D G · Am = C E A · F = C F A

Every move stays ≤1 white key and G→Am stays an all-up-one block-shift — but the
outer voices now move in **sixths, not fifths**, and B→C resolves up. Checked every
transition: no parallel fifths or octaves anywhere in the loop.

## Consequences
- Every common tone now lands on the **same finger** in both chords → glued
  throughout: C→G on G (RH 5 · LH 1); Am→F on **both** C (RH 1 · LH 5) and A
  (RH 5 · LH 1); F→C on C (RH 1 · LH 5). The "held note, re-fingered" case
  (Eduard's own correction, [[0002-note-vs-finger-handrail]]) no longer occurs in
  this loop. Kept the note≠finger distinction in the GLOSSARY as a general
  principle only (his choice over engineering a re-finger to preserve it).
- New per-hand fingering, reproduced by Eduard's rule: RH C/F = 1·3·5, G/Am = 1·2·5;
  LH C/G/Am = 5·3·1, F = 5·2·1.
- Updated this session: lessons/0002 (chips, fingering table, both keyboard SVGs,
  voice-leading table, trainer JS, drills — dropped the held-note drill, added a
  "two handrails" drill for Am→F), reference/0001, GLOSSARY, NOTES, and the note in
  0002.
- The lesson text deliberately says nothing about parallel fifths — only the chord
  forms changed, per Eduard's instruction. Lesson 3's LH bass builds under THESE
  shapes/fingerings.

## Evidence
Eduard's request, stated with the correct theory (parallel fifth at V→vi, leading-
tone resolution) — consistent with his established prior knowledge of chord theory
and inversions. He confirmed the trade-off: keep note≠finger as a glossary principle
and trim the now-inapplicable drill.
