# Prior knowledge established, and the real bottleneck

Eduard arrived knowing how to **build chords** and **where chords are located**
on the keyboard, and he already **uses inversions** to keep his hands in one zone
rather than leaping in root position. So the bottleneck is **not** voice leading
or jump distance — it is **keyboard sense (orientation by feel)**. He loses where
his hands are the instant he stops looking.

Both hands are affected, and the goal is pop/folk accompaniment.

## Implications for future sessions
- Do not teach chord theory, inversion theory, or voice-leading-to-minimize-leaps
  as if new — he has these. Build on them.
- Start with tactile orientation (black-key landmarks, anchor notes, tactile
  fingerprints), then chaining a full progression, then both hands together.
- The feedback loop must be **eyes-closed → verify**, since the failure only
  appears when sight is removed. Verifying with eyes open is the corrective.

## Evidence
Self-reported in the opening session, with an unusually precise self-diagnosis
("I fail jumping from chord to chord without looking"). Confirmed via three
scoping questions: goal = pop/folk accompaniment; positioning = already uses
inversions; hands = both.
