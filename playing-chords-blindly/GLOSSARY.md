# Playing Chords Without Looking — Glossary

Canonical language for this workspace. Lessons adhere to these terms.

## Terms the user already commands

**Root position**:
A chord voiced with its root as the lowest note.
_Avoid_: basic position, normal chord

**Inversion**:
A chord voiced with a note other than the root on the bottom, used to keep the
hand in one region instead of leaping. The user already uses these well.
_Avoid_: flipped chord, rearranged chord

**Black-key group**:
The clusters of 2 and 3 black keys that repeat across the keyboard. The
irregular 2–3 pattern is the landmark you read by touch. _(Promoted 2026-06-12:
mastering the lesson-3 bass drills means finding G and A tucked inside the
3-group by feel.)_
_Avoid_: black note bunch, sharps/flats group

**Anchor note**:
A white key you can locate purely by feel from a black-key group. **C** is just
left of any group of 2; **F** is just left of any group of 3. You navigate to
everything else from an anchor. _(Promoted 2026-06-12 with [[black-key-group]] —
exercised blind throughout lessons 2–3.)_
_Avoid_: reference note, home key

## Terms introduced in this workspace
_(Promote to "commands" once the user demonstrates them in a session.)_

**Keyboard sense (proprioception)**:
Knowing where your hands are on the keyboard without looking — the skill this
whole mission is about.
_Avoid_: muscle memory (related but not the same), feel

**Tactile fingerprint**:
The unique feel of a chord shape under the hand — which fingers rest on raised
(black) keys vs flat (white) keys, and how far apart they sit. Used to confirm a
chord landed correctly without looking.
_Avoid_: chord feel, hand shape

**The gap (seam)**:
The wider stretch of white where there is no black key — between E–F and between
B–C. A tactile landmark in its own right, not just an absence.
_Avoid_: the space, the missing black key

**Voice leading**:
Moving from one chord to the next with each note travelling the smallest possible
distance. The principle behind keeping the hand in one place. (Eduard already
applies this instinctively via [[inversion]]; named here so lessons can refer to
it.)
_Avoid_: smooth changes, leading the notes

**Common tone (the shared note)**:
A note shared by two consecutive chords, so its *key* doesn't move through the
change. For blind play it's a landmark — but it is the shared *note*, not
automatically a shared *finger*. See [[glued-finger]].
_Avoid_: pivot note, held note

**Glued finger (the handrail)**:
A common tone that both chords also play with the **same finger** — so the finger
never leaves the key and the hand pivots around it. The strongest orientation cue,
and the systematic version of Lesson 1's "stationary finger". A common tone gives
a glued finger *only when the fingering matches*. If a chord's shape redistributes
the fingers, the note can stay on its key while the **finger** has to swap — a
**held note, re-fingered**: same key, new finger, still a landmark but not a
"don't-lift" handrail. (The four-chord loop is now voiced so this never arises —
every shared note there keeps its finger — but the distinction still holds for
voicings in general.) Where two chords share nothing (G→Am) there is neither, and
the whole shape shifts by step.
_Avoid_: stationary finger (Lesson-1 informal name), shared note (that's the
common tone, not the finger)

**Chord chart**:
Lyrics with chord symbols written above them, no staff notation — the format
singers and accompanists actually play from, and the page the mission puts the
eyes on. (A *lead sheet* additionally notates the melody; we don't use those yet.)
_Avoid_: sheet music (implies notation), songsheet

**Reading ahead**:
Eyes resting one chord symbol *ahead* of what the hands are playing, so every
change is known before it's due. Eyes lead, hands follow. On a chord chart the
symbols are sparse, so there is always time.
_Avoid_: looking ahead at the music (vague), scanning

**Planned glance**:
A look down at the hands taken at a break *chosen in advance* (line or verse
end), made with the eyes only — head up, back on the page within a beat. The
legitimate exception to eyes-on-the-page. Its opposite, the **panic glance**
(mid-change, head drops, place lost), is never allowed; a fumble is caught by
ear and fixed next lap instead.
_Avoid_: peeking, checking (that's what the ears do now)
