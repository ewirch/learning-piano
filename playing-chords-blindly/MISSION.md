# Mission: Play chords on piano without looking at the keys

## Why
Eduard wants to accompany singing — pop & folk songs — with both hands, keeping
his eyes on the lyrics, the chord chart, or the people he's playing with instead
of on his hands. He can already build chords and uses inversions to keep his
hands in one zone, but he loses his place on the keyboard the moment he stops
looking, so chord changes break down.

## Success looks like
- Play a common pop/folk progression (e.g. C–G–Am–F) with both hands while
  looking at the page, not the keys, without fumbling the chord changes.
- Find any white key by feel using the black-key groups, without looking.
- Confirm a chord is correct by how it *feels* under the fingers, not by sight.
- Sing or read lyrics while comping, eyes off the keys.

## Constraints
- Learning across multiple short sessions.
- Both hands need this — not just one.
- Already knows chord construction and where chords live on the keyboard, and
  already uses inversions. Do **not** re-teach chord theory.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Advanced jazz voicings / reharmonization.
- Sight-reading classical scores.
- Improvising melodies / soloing.
