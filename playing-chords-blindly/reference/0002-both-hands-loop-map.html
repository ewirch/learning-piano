The left-hand bass — found by feel

At each change: what each hand does

ChangeRight hand (chord)Left hand (bass)
C → Gglue G — stillup a 5th → G
G → Amshift up one (no anchor)up one step → A
Am → Fglue C & A — stilldown a 3rd → F
F → Cglue C — stilldown a 4th → C
The one rule: at every change either the right hand is still (lean on it while the bass leaps) or both hands move up one white key together (G→Am — ride the matching motion). You never make two big independent moves at once.

Orient the right hand by its glued finger; orient the left hand by the black-key groups. All chords are white-key only, so the black-vs-white feel won't tell the chords apart — the handrails and the groups do.
