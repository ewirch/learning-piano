RH thumb (1) on the lowest note; LH thumb (1) on the highest. The rule: keep fingers 3 and 5 within a third; let the thumb take any fourth. When the fourth falls on the pinky side, put the middle note on finger 2 (2–5 spans the fourth) — that's RH G and Am (1·2·5) and LH F (5·2·1). Everything else is plain 1·3·5 / 5·3·1.

The one rule: no finger ever moves more than one white key. Three changes give you a glued finger to ride (C→G on G, Am→F on both C and A, F→C on C); only G → Am gives no anchor at all, so shift the whole shape up one white key as a block — the uniform motion is the cue.

All four chords are white-key only, so you orient by the glued finger (where you get one), by the block-shift's uniform motion (where you don't), and by the black-key groups around your hand — not by a black-vs-white feel.
