# The moves hold keys, not fingers — Eduard's re-finger grid for 3rd-apart changes

Lesson 1 originally branded the 3rd-apart move "glue two (fingers)". Eduard
rejected the name on ergonomic grounds: fingers 3 and 5 never cover more than a
3rd, so whenever a change reshapes the chord across that limit, a held key must
re-finger — his prior-workspace "held note, re-fingered" distinction
(`../playing-chords-blindly/learning-records/0002-note-vs-finger-handrail.md`),
now applied to *my own rule-naming*. He stated the direction-dependence himself:
the both-fingers-glue outcome holds for the RH on rising roots and for the LH on
falling roots (from root-position frames).

## Verified mechanics (worked through all 6 rotation × direction combos per hand)

A held key keeps its finger **iff the fingering pattern survives the change**.
RH patterns (1·3·5 / 1·2·5 / 1·3·5 by position) flip exactly when a change
enters or leaves a **1st-inversion** shape; LH patterns (5·3·1 / 5·3·1 / 5·2·1)
flip at **2nd-inversion** shapes. The flip lands on a *held* key (forcing a
3↔2 swap under it) in exactly two cells per hand:

| hold-two from… | RH, root ↑3rd | RH, root ↓3rd | LH, root ↑3rd | LH, root ↓3rd |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| root position  | both glue | **middle held, 3→2** | **middle held, 3→2** | both glue |
| 1st inversion  | **middle held, 2→3** | both glue | both glue | both glue |
| 2nd inversion  | both glue | both glue | both glue | **middle held, 2→3** |

So his root-position statement is exact (RH rising / LH falling), and the full
grid adds an always-safe diagonal: RH from 2nd inversion, LH from 1st inversion.
Hold-one changes obey the same pass (the single shared key can sit in a flipping
middle); mirror changes hold nothing. His fingering rule is confirmed as the
standard taught fingering (pianoguidelessons.com, in RESOURCES).

## Implications
- Never name or describe a move as gluing **fingers**; moves operate on keys.
  Teaching frame is two passes: move → keys, fingering rule → fingers.
- Renamed across the workspace: glue two/one → **hold two/one**; GLOSSARY now
  defines the **second pass** and reserves "glue" for key+finger (as the prior
  workspace already did — the original sin was violating our own glossary).
- Third teacher-correction in a row that survived verification. His ergonomic
  constraints are hard constraints, not preferences.

## Evidence
His message of 2026-06-12 ("Finger 5 and 3 never cover distances more than a
3rd"), the prior-workspace fingering rule, and the verification grid above.
