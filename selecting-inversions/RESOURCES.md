# Selecting Inversions — Resources

## Knowledge

- [26.7 Voice Leading Root Position Triads in Four Parts — *Music Theory for the 21st-Century Classroom*, Robert Hutchinson](https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/VoiceLeadingFourPartsRootPosition.html)
  The backbone of this workspace. Gives the exact procedures keyed by bass
  motion: roots a 3rd/6th apart → retain two common tones, move the other voice
  by step; roots a 4th/5th apart → keep the shared tone, move the rest stepwise;
  roots a 2nd apart → upper parts in contrary motion to the bass. Also: always
  double the root of root-position triads, and the V–vi exception (3rd of V up a
  step, doubling the 3rd of vi). Use for: the three moves (lesson 1), the
  deceptive change (lesson 2).

- [26.3 Objectionable Parallels — *Music Theory for the 21st-Century Classroom*, Robert Hutchinson](https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/ObjectionableParallels.html)
  The rulebook for what's forbidden: no two voices in parallel 5ths, octaves, or
  unisons (octave-compounds count); parallel 3rds, 4ths, 6ths acceptable; the
  why (independence of voices). Use for: stating the rules without re-teaching
  them — he already commands the concept.

- [26.8 Voice Leading First-Inversion Triads — *Music Theory for the 21st-Century Classroom*, Robert Hutchinson](https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/VoiceLeadingFirstInversionTriads.html)
  Doubling and connection rules once the *bass* leaves the root. Use for: the
  later slash-chord / bass-inversion chapter of the mission. Not yet used.

- [Common Tone-Stepwise and Contrary Motion-Nearest — G Major Music Theory (PDF)](https://www.gmajormusictheory.org/Fundamentals/Ch15.pdf)
  The same two procedures under their classroom names (CTS / CMN), with the
  decision rule "any common tone → CTS; none → CMN". Use for: a second framing
  if the move table ever needs reinforcement.

- [Fingering Chords on Piano — Piano Guide Lessons](https://pianoguidelessons.com/fingering-chords-on-piano/)
  The standard triad fingerings per position: RH 1·3·5 (root), 1·2·5 (1st inv),
  1·3·5 (2nd inv); LH 5·3·1, 5·3·1, 5·2·1. Independently confirms Eduard's
  generative fingering rule. Use for: the fingers second pass (lesson 1,
  learning-record 0002), and any future shape's fingering.

- ['50s progression — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%2750s_progression)
  I–vi–IV–V, the "Stand by Me changes": Earth Angel, Stand by Me, Every Breath
  You Take, Perfect, Blank Space, and a long song list; variants (I–vi–ii–V,
  I–IV–vi–V). Use for: grounding lesson 1's progression in songs he'd actually
  comp, and a menu for transfer lessons.

- [I–V–vi–IV progression — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I%E2%80%93V%E2%80%93vi%E2%80%93IV_progression) ⤺
  His blind-play loop's progression and its song list. Use for: transfer
  material, and lesson 2's repair of the G→Am change.
