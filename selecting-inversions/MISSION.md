# Mission: Select chord inversions correctly and efficiently

## Why
When Eduard opens the chord chart of a new pop song, he wants to derive each
chord's shape on the fly — not look up or precompute voicings, and not have a
teacher re-voice progressions *for* him (as happened with the G→Am parallel-fifth
fix in the playing-chords-blindly workspace). "Correctly" means classic
four-voice part-writing standards — no parallel fifths, no parallel octaves,
leading tone resolved. "Efficiently" means the decision happens at playing
speed, and the hand stays in one comfortable zone.

## Success looks like
- Given an unfamiliar diatonic pop progression (root letters off a chart),
  derive each next chord's shape in real time — no trial-and-error across
  three inversions.
- Spot **and fix** a parallel-fifth/octave trap (IV→V, V→vi) unaided.
- Keep the right hand inside a hand-sized box, each voice moving the least the
  rules allow, with his fingering rule applied without thought.
- Explain *why* a chosen shape is correct, in rulebook terms.

## Constraints
- Multiple short sessions.
- Texture is his established one: **RH close triad + LH bass root** (four
  voices, keyboard style). Don't change it under him.
- Build on, never conflict with, the playing-chords-blindly workspace (same
  B3–A4 box, same fingering rule). The two skills must compose: derive a
  voicing here, play it blind there.
- Do **not** re-teach chord construction, inversion theory, or what a parallel
  fifth is — he has all of these (see learning-records/0001).

## Out of scope (for now)
- Seventh chords, extensions, jazz voicings.
- SATB choral writing for its own sake — keyboard style only.
- Slash-chord bass lines (bass stays on roots until upper-voice selection is
  automatic; bass-note inversions are a later chapter of this same mission).
- Deliberate rule-breaking as a pop style choice — revisit only after the
  classic habit is solid (his explicit request: build the classic habit).
