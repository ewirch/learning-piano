# Selecting Inversions — Glossary

Canonical language for this workspace. Lessons adhere to these terms.
Terms marked ⤺ are carried over from the playing-chords-blindly workspace.

## Terms the user already commands

**Inversion** ⤺:
A chord voiced with a note other than the root on the bottom. In this workspace
the word usually means the *right-hand close-triad arrangement*; see
[[keyboard style]] for why the harmony's true inversion is set by the bass.
_Avoid_: flipped chord

**Voice leading** ⤺:
Moving from one chord to the next with each voice travelling the smallest
distance the rules allow.
_Avoid_: smooth changes

**Common tone (the shared note)** ⤺:
A note shared by two consecutive chords. The number of common tones is fixed by
the [[root interval]] — that fact powers the whole move table.
_Avoid_: pivot note

**Glued finger (the handrail)** ⤺:
A common tone that both chords play with the same finger, so the finger never
leaves the key. When a shape redistributes fingers, the note can stay while the
finger swaps — a **held note, re-fingered** (e.g. E at C→Am in the '50s loop:
key stays, finger 3→2).
_Avoid_: stationary finger

**Parallel fifths / parallel octaves**:
Two voices a perfect fifth (or octave/unison, in any register compounding)
apart that move to *another* fifth (or octave) in the same direction. Forbidden
between **any** pair of the four voices, bass included. Parallel 3rds, 4ths and
6ths are fine. Demonstrated: he flagged the V→vi parallels in the prior
workspace unprompted.
_Avoid_: consecutive fifths (same thing, just pick one term)

**Leading tone**:
Scale degree 7 (B in C major). When it sounds in a V chord moving to I, it
resolves up by step to the tonic.
_Avoid_: leading note

## Terms introduced in this workspace
_(Promote to "commands" once the user demonstrates them in a session.)_

**Keyboard style**:
Four-voice texture laid out for piano: three upper voices close together in the
right hand, bass in the left. His established RH-triad + LH-root texture *is*
keyboard style. With the root in the bass the harmony is always root position —
what he "selects" is the rotation of the three upper voices.
_Avoid_: SATB (that's the choral layout, not this)

**Root interval**:
The interval between the *roots* of two consecutive chords, read straight off a
chord chart (letter distance). It alone determines the number of common tones:
3rd/6th apart → two; 4th/5th apart → one; 2nd/7th apart → none.
_Avoid_: chord distance

**The three moves (the move table)**:
This workspace's name for the classic part-writing procedures, keyed by root
interval — [[hold two]], [[hold one]], [[mirror the bass]]. The selection rule
of the whole mission: *count the root interval, apply the move, and the
inversion chooses itself.* The moves decide **keys only**; fingers follow in
the [[second pass]].

**Hold two** (roots a 3rd apart):
Both shared keys stay down, the one remaining voice steps. Whether each held
key keeps its finger is the [[second pass]]'s question.
_Textbook name_: common-tone & stepwise connection.
_Avoid_: glue two (overpromises — a held key is not automatically a glued finger)

**Hold one** (roots a 4th/5th apart):
The shared key stays down, the other two voices step to the nearest chord
tones.
_Textbook name_: common-tone & stepwise connection.
_Avoid_: glue one (same overpromise)

**Second pass (the fingers pass)**:
After the move picks the keys, finger the destination shape by Eduard's rule
(3-and-5 within a third; thumb takes any fourth; pinky-side fourth puts the
middle on 2 — which is exactly the standard taught triad fingering). A held key
keeps its finger **only if the fingering pattern survives the change**. RH
patterns flip when a change enters or leaves a 1st-inversion shape (LH: a
2nd-inversion shape); a held *middle* key then re-fingers 3↔2 underneath —
a held note, re-fingered, never a [[glued finger]]. From root position: RH
keeps both fingers on *rising* 3rds, LH on *falling* 3rds; RH from 2nd
inversion and LH from 1st inversion always keep both.

**Mirror the bass** (roots a 2nd apart):
No common tone exists. Move all three right-hand voices in the direction
*opposite* the bass, each to its nearest chord tone. The same-direction
shortcut (block shift with the bass) is the [[parallel trap]].
_Textbook name_: contrary-motion & nearest connection.

**Parallel trap**:
A change where the lazy move (everything shifts the same way as the bass)
creates parallel fifths/octaves against the bass. Lives exactly where roots are
a 2nd apart: IV→V, V→vi. The chord you land on isn't wrong — the road is.
_Avoid_: forbidden chord (it's the motion that's forbidden, never the chord)

**Doubling**:
Which chord member appears twice among the four voices. With the bass on the
root and a complete triad in the right hand, the root is doubled automatically —
the textbook default. Becomes a live choice at the V→vi exception (lesson 2
territory) and in later bass-inversion work.
_Avoid_: duplication
