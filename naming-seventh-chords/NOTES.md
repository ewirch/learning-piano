## The four-vs-five "diminished" point (RAISE in lesson 2, don't bury)
He listed **four** qualities: major-7, minor-7, dominant-7, **diminished-7**.
There are **five** — he omitted the **half-diminished (ø7, m7♭5)**. And a real
subtlety to clarify with him:
- In a **major** key the diatonic chord on degree 7 is **half-diminished**
  (e.g. Bø7 = B–D–F–A), *not* fully-diminished.
- The **fully-diminished °7** (e.g. B°7 = B–D–F–A♭) is **chromatic** in major
  (it comes from harmonic minor / borrowing).
- So "the diminished one" is genuinely two chords. Lesson 2 teaches both and asks
  which he meant / cares about for the pop-folk repertoire. Don't silently pick.
Source: MT21C *Roman Numerals of Diatonic Seventh Chords*; OMT 1.1.

**Status: DELIVERED in Lesson 2**, and the four-vs-five question is now **RESOLVED —
but not as I'd framed it.** He replied (2026-06-18): *"I named only four 7th chords,
because I'm still learning and didn't know about the half-diminished."* So it was a
**knowledge gap, not a conflation and not a preference** — he wasn't picking °7 over
ø7, he simply hadn't met ø7. My old "which did you mean?" framing was wrong; drop it.

**Calibration this gives us (important):** his *playing* floor (triads, inversions,

of a **beginner** — "still learning", his own words. **Do not assume naming/spelling

verifying against what he actually knows, not what a fluent player "should" know.

asks. This is the "understand the harmony" payoff he signed up for.
