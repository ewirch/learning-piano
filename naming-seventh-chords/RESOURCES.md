# Naming Seventh Chords — Resources

## Knowledge

- [8.1 Introduction to Seventh Chords — *Music Theory for the 21st-Century Classroom*, R. Hutchinson](https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/SeventhChordsIntroduction.html)
  The five qualities (major, major-minor/dominant, minor, half-diminished,
  fully-diminished) and the "built in thirds" framing — each quality is a
  different stack of major/minor thirds. Use for: the anatomy (lesson 1) and the
  five qualities (lesson 2).

- [Open Music Theory — 1.1 Triads and Seventh Chords (LibreTexts ed.)](https://human.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Music/Music_Theory/Open_Music_Theory_1e_(Wharton_and_Shaffer_Eds)/01:_Fundamentals/1.01:_Triads_and_Seventh_Chords)
  The cleanest single table of **symbols + interval recipes + stacked-thirds**
  for all five qualities: maj7/△7 (M3·m3·M3), 7 (M3·m3·m3), m7 (m3·M3·m3),
  ø7 (m3·m3·M3), °7/dim7 (m3·m3·m3). Use for: nomenclature and spelling (the
  whole workspace's reference for *symbols*).

- [Roman Numerals of Diatonic Seventh Chords — MT21C, R. Hutchinson](https://musictheory.pugetsound.edu/mt21c/RomanNumeralsOfDiatonicSeventhChords.html)
  Which quality lands on each scale degree of a major key: Imaj7, ii m7, iii m7,
  IVmaj7, V7, vi m7, **viiø7** — and why degree 7 is *half*-diminished, not fully.
  Use for: seventh chords in a key (lesson 3) and the four-vs-five clarification.

## Practice (automated feedback loops)

- [musictheory.net — Chord Identification](https://www.musictheory.net/exercises/chord) and [Chord Construction](https://www.musictheory.net/exercises/chord-construction)
  Shows a chord on a staff → you name it (identification), or names a chord → you
  build it on a staff (construction). Both can be **customised to seventh chords
  only** via [the customizer](https://www.musictheory.net/exercises/customize).
  This is the tightest possible loop for *exactly* this mission — both directions,
  instant right/wrong. Use for: between-lesson drilling.

- [teoria.com — Chord exercises](https://www.teoria.com/en/exercises/)
  Chord construction and identification with staff notation; a second source if
  musictheory.net ever feels stale. Use for: variety in drilling.
