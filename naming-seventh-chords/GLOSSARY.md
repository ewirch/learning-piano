# Naming Seventh Chords — Glossary

Canonical language for this workspace. Lessons adhere to these terms. Terms
marked ⤺ are carried over from the playing workspaces (he already commands them).

## Terms the user already commands

**Triad** ⤺:
A three-note chord built in stacked thirds — root, third, fifth.

**Root** ⤺:
The note a chord is built on and named after. When the chord is stacked in thirds
it is the bottom note (see [[root position]]).
_Avoid_: base note, tonic (tonic is a key's home note, not a chord's root)

**Root position** ⤺:
A chord voiced with its root as the lowest note. This workspace stays in root
position throughout — [[inversion]] is out of scope for now.
_Avoid_: basic position

**Inversion** ⤺:
A chord voiced with a note other than the root on the bottom. Named here only to
mark it out of scope: until naming is automatic, every chord is root position.

**Third** ⤺:
The interval that skips one letter (C→E, D→F). **Major third** = 4 half-steps,
**minor third** = 3. The brick every chord in this workspace is built from.

## Terms introduced in this workspace
_(Promote to "commands" once he uses them correctly in a session.)_

**Seventh chord**:
A four-note chord — a [[triad]] plus one more [[third]] stacked on top, which adds
a note a seventh above the [[root]]. Its [[quality]] — which of the five — is
decided by the accidentals (see [[the five qualities]]).
_Avoid_: 7 chord (ambiguous — "7" alone means the *dominant* seventh)

**The seventh (chordal seventh)**:
The fourth note of a [[seventh chord]], a seventh above the root — the note that
turns a triad into a seventh chord.
_Avoid_: the 7th note (sounds like a scale degree)

**Built in thirds (tertian)**:
Stacked entirely in thirds. A root-position seventh chord is four notes, each a
third above the one below it.

**The stack (the snowman)**:
The visual signature of a root-position seventh chord on the staff — four
noteheads stacked in thirds, sitting **all on lines** or **all on spaces**.
_Avoid_: blob, cluster

**The skeleton**:
The four *letter-names* of a seventh chord (root · third · fifth · seventh), read
before any accidentals — e.g. the skeleton **C–E–G–B** underlies both Cmaj7 and
C7. Lesson 1's level: get the skeleton; Lesson 2 adds the accidentals that decide
quality.
_Avoid_: outline, the notes (too vague once quality matters)

**Treble-clef anchor (the G line)**:
The treble clef's curl wraps the **second line from the bottom**, which is G4.
That is the landmark you read every other line and space from — the staff cousin
of a keyboard **anchor note**.
_Avoid_: the G clef line (wordy)

**Alphabet-skip**:
Spelling a stack of thirds by reading **every other letter** of the musical
alphabet from the root up: C→E→G→B, D→F→A→C. Find the bottom note, skip-skip-skip.
_Avoid_: counting thirds (the skip *is* the third; one motion, one name)

## The five qualities (introduced Lesson 2)

**Quality**:
*Which* seventh chord a [[skeleton]] is, once the accidentals are set — read as a
[[triad]] (major / minor / diminished) **plus which seventh** sits on top (major,
minor, or diminished 7th). The whole point of naming; five of them are in common use.
_Avoid_: type, kind (fine in passing, but "quality" is the term sources use)

**The five qualities**:
The named seventh chords, brightest to darkest: **major 7th**, **dominant 7th**,
**minor 7th**, **half-diminished 7th**, **fully-diminished 7th**. They line up as
[[the staircase]] — each lowers one more note a half-step than the last.

**Major seventh (maj7, △7)**:
Major [[triad]] + a *major* seventh (a half-step under the octave). Recipe in thirds
M·m·M. On C: C–E–G–B. The bright, resolved one.

**Dominant seventh (7)**:
Major [[triad]] + a *minor* seventh. Recipe M·m·m. On C: C–E–G–B♭. The bare symbol
"7" means *this* chord — never a major 7th.
_Avoid_: writing "Cmaj7" when you mean C7, or vice versa

**Minor seventh (m7, −7)**:
Minor [[triad]] + a minor seventh. Recipe m·M·m. On C: C–E♭–G–B♭.

**Half-diminished seventh (ø7, m7♭5)**:
Diminished [[triad]] + a *minor* seventh (only the triad is diminished — hence
"half"). Recipe m·m·M. On C: C–E♭–G♭–B♭. This is the diatonic "diminished" chord in
a major key (on degree 7). The alias **m7♭5** literally reads its recipe.
_Avoid_: calling it "diminished" unqualified (that collapses it with [[fully-diminished seventh (°7, dim7)]])

**Fully-diminished seventh (°7, dim7)**:
Diminished [[triad]] + a *diminished* seventh — the seventh is lowered too, so spelled
in thirds it needs a **double-flat** (C°7 = C–E♭–G♭–B♭♭, the B stays a B). Recipe
m·m·m (all minor thirds, symmetric). Chromatic in major; comes from the minor side.

**The staircase (the lowering ladder)**:
The memory picture for [[the five qualities]]: start at the major 7th and lower one
note a half-step at a time — **maj7 → 7 → m7 → ø7 → °7** — same four letters
throughout, the chord darkening at each step. To *write* a quality fast: spell the
maj7, then add the flats the staircase shows.
_Avoid_: "the cycle" (it's a one-way descent, not a loop)
