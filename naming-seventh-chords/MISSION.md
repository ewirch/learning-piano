# Mission: Name and read seventh chords on the staff

## Why
When Eduard looks at the harmony of a song, he wants to **understand** it — to
see a seventh chord written on the staff and name it (root + quality), and to
write any named seventh chord onto the staff from scratch. The payoff is
comprehension, not speed: grasping how these four-note chords are built and what
makes a maj7 different from a dom7 — the vocabulary underneath the pop/folk songs
he already plays. (Later this can feed back into richer accompaniment; for now
the goal is to *read and name*, not to comp.)

## Success looks like
- Given a **root-position seventh chord on the treble staff**, name it
  unaided — e.g. G–B–D–F → **G7** — both the root and the quality.
- Given a **seventh-chord name** (Cmaj7, Aø7, B♭7…), write its four notes on the
  staff in root position, with correct accidentals.
- Explain the difference between the five qualities (major, dominant, minor,
  half-diminished, fully-diminished) in terms of **triad + which seventh**.
- Say which quality lands on each degree of a major scale, and why the chord on
  scale-degree 7 is *half*-diminished.

## Constraints
- Multiple short sessions.
- He reads **chord charts** fluently but has **not sight-read staff notation**
  (it was explicitly out of scope before). So the minimal slice of staff-reading
  needed to decode one stacked chord is itself part of this mission — taught as
  "decode a chord", **not** "learn to sight-read", and anchored on his existing
  keyboard **anchor-note** instinct.
- Build on his command of triads, thirds, intervals, root position, and
  inversion (from the playing workspaces) — do **not** re-teach these.
- One thing per lesson; tight, ideally automatic feedback loops. He corrects
  teachers precisely and is right — verify, then adopt.

## Out of scope (for now)
- **Inversions** of seventh chords and figured bass (7 / 6-5 / 4-3 / 4-2) — root
  position only until naming is automatic (his explicit "no inversions yet").
- Sight-reading at tempo, and rhythm reading.
- Voicing / comping these chords at the keyboard — that is the playing
  workspaces' territory; this mission is about naming and understanding.
- Ninth/eleventh/thirteenth chords and other extensions.
- Bass clef — start in treble; add it when the naming skill is solid.
