# Floor imported; this topic is staff-reading for harmonic understanding

Eduard arrives with the full floor of the playing workspaces — triads,
intervals/thirds, root position, inversion, voice leading — so chord *structure*
is not new. What is new is the **modality and the goal**, both established at
kickoff:

- **Modality = the staff.** Asked what "music notation" meant, he chose
  *noteheads on the staff* (not letter-names, not keys). He has **not sight-read**
  before (it was out of scope in the playing workspaces), so the minimal slice of
  treble-staff reading needed to decode one stacked chord is itself in scope —
  taught as "decode a chord", anchored on his keyboard anchor-note instinct.
- **Goal = understand the harmony.** Asked for the payoff, he chose *naming and
  spelling to understand how songs are built* — not comping. So this workspace is
  analytical: name a written chord, write a named chord, explain the qualities.

## Implications
- Start at **structure on the staff** (the stacked-thirds skeleton), not at chord
  theory he already owns. Lesson 1 = the skeleton; quality is deferred to lesson 2
  (skeleton-before-flesh).
- Lessons must render real staff notation and pair it with a keyboard he knows.
- His four named qualities omit the **half-diminished**, and his "diminished"
  conflates the diatonic-in-major (half-dim) with the chromatic fully-dim — a
  point to surface, not bury (lesson 2).

## Open / to calibrate

way; his report on it sets whether a pure staff-landmark drill is needed before
